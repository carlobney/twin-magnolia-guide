Louisiana Cottage Food Law

Act 542 (2014) allows home-based production without a commercial kitchen.

Allowed Products

  • Breads, cakes, cookies, pies
  • Candies & confections
  • Dried mixes
  • Jams, jellies, preserves
  • Honey products
  • Pickles (pH < 4.6)
  • Sauces & syrups
  • Spices
Good news: Custard and cream-filled items ARE allowed in Louisiana ‚Äî use pasteurized milk and follow temp requirements.

Restrictions for Baked Goods

  • No employees for production
  • No pets in prep area ‚Äî ever
  • Fridge at 45¬∞F or below
  • Direct to consumer only (no resale)

Cold Brew ‚Äî Permit Options

Coffee is NOT a cottage food. Even cold brew requires food service permitting.

Option A: Mobile Food Permit

  • Full mobile vendor license
  • May require commissary
  • Plans review: $100-1,000
  • Food Safety Cert required

Best for: Frequent events, future expansion

Option B: Temp Event Permits

  • Per-event permits
  • Lower upfront cost
  • More paperwork each time
  • May have frequency limits

Best for: Testing the market

The Commissary Requirement

This is often the biggest hurdle for small mobile vendors.

What Is a Commissary?

A licensed commercial kitchen for:

  • Food prep & storage
  • Equipment cleaning/sanitizing
  • Potable water supply
  • Wastewater disposal
The problem: Commissary access costs $200-800/month ‚Äî which destroys the economics of 5-10 gallons of cold brew per week.

Why Cold Brew Is Different

Commissary rules target food trucks with grills, fryers, raw meat. Cold brew is just coffee + water, steeped cold, filtered ‚Äî no more complex than making iced tea.

Questions to Ask LDH

Be specific. Get answers in writing (email follow-up).

  1. "Does cold brew prepared at home and sold at events require a commissary?"
  2. "Is there a 'limited food prep' exemption for cold beverages?"
  3. "Can I use temporary event permits without a commissary for cold-only service?"
  4. "What if I sell sealed, pre-bottled cold brew instead of pouring?"
  5. "Are there different rules for operations under $30K/year?"
After the call: Send an email ‚Äî "Per our conversation, I understand [X]. Please confirm." Get it in writing.

Dairy/Non-Dairy Separation

Production Protocol

  • Dedicate days for dairy-free
  • Separate utensils & pans
  • Clean between batches
  • Separate ingredient storage

Labeling Protocol

  • Clear "DAIRY FREE" marking
  • List all allergens
  • Color-coded labels
  • "Made in facility that processes dairy"
Keep a production log showing when dairy vs. non-dairy batches were made. Protects you and demonstrates due diligence.
