Louisiana Cottage Food Law

Louisiana Act 542 (2014) allows home-based production of certain foods without a commercial kitchen.

What's Allowed

  • Baked goods: breads, cakes, cookies, pies
  • Candies and confections
  • Dried mixes
  • Jams, jellies, preserves
  • Honey and honeycomb products
  • Pickles and acidified foods (pH below 4.6)
  • Sauces and syrups
  • Spices
Good news: Custard and cream-filled items ARE allowed in Louisiana (unlike most states) ‚Äî just use pasteurized milk products and follow temp requirements.

Restrictions for Breads/Cakes/Cookies/Pies

  • Cannot employ anyone to help with production
  • No pets allowed in preparation area ‚Äî ever
  • Refrigerator must maintain 45¬∞F or below
  • Cannot sell for resale (direct to consumer only)

Cold Brew Coffee ‚Äî What You Need

Coffee is NOT a cottage food. Even cold brew (no electricity, served cold) requires food service permitting because it's a beverage prepared for public consumption.

Options for Coffee Service

Option A: Mobile Food Establishment

Get permitted as a mobile food vendor with LDH.

  • Requires approved commissary (shared commercial kitchen for prep/storage)
  • Plans review: $100 standard / $1,000 expedited
  • Annual permit fee: varies by parish
  • Food Safety Certification required

Best for: Full coffee service with hot options later

Option B: Temporary Event Permits

Get temporary permits for each event/market.

  • Per-event permits from parish health dept
  • Lower upfront cost
  • More paperwork per event
  • May have limitations on frequency

Best for: Testing the market, fewer events

Cold Brew Specifics

Cold brew concentrate is typically considered a Time/Temperature Control for Safety (TCS) food because:

  • It's a low-acid beverage (pH above 4.6)
  • Requires refrigeration at 41¬∞F or below
  • Has a limited shelf life (typically 7-14 days refrigerated)

Dairy/Non-Dairy Separation

Managing allergens is critical for safety and liability.

Separate Production

  • Dedicate specific days or times for dairy-free production
  • Use separate mixing bowls, utensils, and baking pans
  • Thoroughly clean all surfaces between batches
  • Store dairy and non-dairy ingredients separately

Labeling Protocol

  • Clearly mark "DAIRY FREE" on non-dairy products
  • List all allergens on every label
  • Consider color-coded labels (green = dairy-free)
  • Include "Made in a facility that also processes dairy"
Documentation: Keep a production log showing when dairy vs. non-dairy batches were made. This protects you in case of any claims and demonstrates due diligence.

For Cold Brew

Offer dairy-free milk alternatives (oat, almond, coconut) with clearly labeled options. Use separate pitchers for each type.
